Blockchain Seminar

Seminar Overview

This seminar examined the foundations and evolution of blockchain, originally introduced by Satoshi Nakamoto as a decentralised system for secure, transparent transactions (Nakamoto, 2008). It explored key applications across finance, healthcare, supply chains, and digital identity, highlighting blockchain's potential to enhance trust and efficiency (Edwards, 2024).

Core concepts such as decentralisation, immutability, and consensus mechanisms were discussed alongside key challenges, including scalability, energy consumption, security risks, and regulatory complexity. The seminar also presented emerging solutions such as layer-2 scaling, cross-chain interoperability, and privacy-enhancing techniques.

Overall, blockchain was positioned as a transformative technology shaping the future of digital systems and trust in the global economy.
